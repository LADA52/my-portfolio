About Me

Hey, I’m Yasein — a Computer & Communications Engineering student at Alexandria University (expected graduation 2027). My passion for engineering began at 13, when I built my first drone by experimenting with circuits and robotics before I even learned to code. That moment shaped how I see challenges: not as obstacles, but as opportunities to create. A year later, I was selected by the Ministry of Communications for a national program preparing top talent for international programming contests. Since then, I’ve grown from hardware tinkering to software innovation, developing a strong foundation in programming (C, Java) and focusing on data analysis, machine learning, and AI. I enjoy turning raw data into insights and creating AI-driven applications with real-world impact, particularly in healthcare and automation. I thrive both independently and in teams. Collaboration sharpens my communication and leadership skills, while solo projects push my focus and creativity. Beyond academics, I’ve volunteered with large initiatives and organizations like AYDA, leading events and supporting medical campaigns. These experiences taught me how to deliver under pressure, communicate effectively, and make a difference at scale. What excites me most is leaving a personal fingerprint on every project — building solutions that are innovative, reliable, and designed to truly matter.
